Hi, I'm Rodrigo Morales.

I'm a Post-doc fellow at Concordia University with Profr. Yann-Gaël Guéhéneuc at Ptidej Lab. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from Ecole Polytechnique de Montreal , under the supervision of Foutse Khomh, Francisco Chicano and Giuliano Antoniol. My research interests are Software Design quality, Refactoring, Anti-patterns, Search-Based Software engineering, Software development on Mobile apps and Internet of Things (IoT) embedded devices.

My Bio.

I'm a Software Engineering researcher currently pursuing my postdoctoral studies at Concordia University, developing new methods to improve the quality of software development for the Internet of Things. I obtained my BS. degree in computer science in 2005 from Polytechnic of Mexico. In 2008, I obtained my MsC. in computer technology from the same University, where I also worked as a Professor in the computer Science department for five years. I've also worked in the bank industry as a software developer for more than three years. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in computer engineering at Polytechnique Montreal where I was awarded best thesis of 2017. I've disseminated my research work through the top software engineering Journals including IEEE TSE, ESEM, and JSS, and top conferences like ICSE, and SANER. I am one of the main organizers of the 1st International Workshop on Software Engineering Research and Practices for the Internet of Things (SERP4IoT), co-located with ICSE 2019, and actively participate as committee member of ICPC conference.
